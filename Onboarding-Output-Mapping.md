# Clarity 2.0 — Onboarding to Output Mapping

**What this is**

For each of the 6 onboarding questions I have written what Clara learns from it and how it changes the Today screen the Create flow the persona and what happens if the user skips it. This is the logic the dev team builds from.

**One thing first**

Onboarding is not a form that has to be perfect. Most answers are single tap or filled in for the user. Q3 is the only real typing. Clara reads what she can and shows it back on the plan screen so the user can fix it. She reads and confirms. She does not make the user fill everything in.

---

## Q1. What kind of business are you growing?

Small business. Online store. Service based. SaaS or tech. Creator. Agency. Nonprofit. Other.

**Clara learns** what type of business it is. Product or service or software or audience. This sets the lane for everything else.

**Today screen** shows the right kind of moves. A shop gets product and seasonal ideas. A service gets trust and proof ideas. SaaS gets problem and time saving ideas. A creator gets story ideas.

**Create flow** picks the default platform and angle. Shop leans Instagram. Service leans Instagram and Google. SaaS leans LinkedIn. Creator leans YouTube.

**Persona** starts from a matching template. Shop is the careful browser. Service is the cautious researcher. SaaS is the busy operator. Creator is the learner.

**If skipped** or set to Other or Small business Clara does not lock a type. She leans on Q3 instead. If the Q3 sentence says bakery she treats it like a bakery even if the type was left generic.

---

## Q2. What do you want Clarity to help with first?

More leads. More sales. Understand customers. Better content. Launch something. Test an idea. Keep up with rivals. Build a plan.

**Clara learns** the main goal right now. This is what the first move aims at.

**Today screen** sets the first move and the order of the day. Leads gets a simple intro move. Sales gets a push on the best offer. Content gets a strong post to share. Rivals gets a move on the gap they miss. Understand gets a customer insight move instead of a sell.

**Create flow** shapes the angle. Sales is offer led. Content is story led. Rivals is contrast led. Leads is problem then answer.

**Persona** does not build the persona but it decides which motivation to lead with. Sales leans on the ready to buy side. Understand opens up more of the audience to look at.

**If skipped** or set to build a plan Clara defaults to leads and treats the whole plan as the job. Test an idea also keeps the budget low and stays organic.

---

## Q3. Who is your ideal customer and what do you sell them?

**Clara learns** the most from this one. The customer and the offer together. From it she works out the type the goal the audience the location if a city is named and the actual thing she writes about. She keeps what the user said apart from what she guessed.

**Today screen** gets sharper or softer based on this. A full answer gives moves that name the real offer and buyer. A thin answer gives general moves and Clara marks the parts she is guessing.

**Create flow** uses this to make posts specific. The offer becomes the subject. The customer becomes who it is written for. Without it the content stays generic.

**Persona** is built mostly from here. Clara makes the persona name and context and motivation from this answer and tags each part as said or guessed so the guesses are visible.

**If thin** like young people who like coffee Clara does not block or count words. She builds her best guess persona marks the guessed parts and lets the first post and the results screen catch and fix anything wrong. If it is empty she uses the default persona and asks nothing until she has shown value.

---

## Q4. Where are you marketing right now?

Website. Instagram. Facebook. TikTok. LinkedIn. Email. Google Ads. Meta Ads. SEO or blog. In person. Referrals. Not marketing yet.

**Clara learns** where the user already puts effort. Where they are now not where they should be.

**Today screen** sends moves to the channels they actually use. If they are on Instagram and Email the moves go there. Clara can also point out a useful gap as its own tip.

**Create flow** uses this to pick the platform for the first draft. In the build Create makes the draft for the first channel they picked instead of a default one.

**Persona** gets a light hint here. The channel mix suggests where the audience can be reached.

**If they pick** Not marketing yet it clears the rest. Clara then suggests a good first channel and frames Today around setting it up. If skipped she uses the default channels for the type.

---

## Q5. What monthly budget should Clarity plan around?

0 to 250. 250 to 1k. 1k to 5k. 5k to 15k. 15k plus. Not sure yet.

**Clara learns** whether to plan for free channels only or paid too and how big paid can go.

**Today screen** shows or hides paid moves. A low budget keeps Today organic. 1k and up unlocks paid tests next to the organic moves. Nothing paid shows up below a real budget.

**Create flow** changes format. A bigger budget brings ad ready formats. A small budget stays with normal posts.

**Persona** is barely touched here. Clara does not read a persona from a budget number.

**If not sure** or skipped Clara stays organic first and never suggests paid until a budget is set.

---

## Q6. Where is your business based?

**Clara learns** the location for local ideas timing and market signals. In the build this comes pre filled. First from a city named in Q3 and if not from the device. A small note tells the user it was recognised and the note goes away once they edit it.

**Today screen** gets local moves and the right timing. Local shops get near me ideas and local events. The best time to post uses the local time zone. For an online business location matters less for content and more for timing.

**Create flow** gives local businesses geo tagged and in city angles and posts get scheduled on local time. Online businesses skip the local angles.

**Persona** gets more context. A regular in Leeds reads differently from one in London. It also sets up future local and supplier features.

**If wrong** the user edits it and the note clears. If skipped Clara plans without local signals uses a sensible time zone and keeps the local features off until a location is set.

---

**In short**

Every answer either sets the lane Q1 and Q2 gives the real material Q3 or sets the limits to plan inside Q4 Q5 Q6. The only three Clara cannot guess are channels budget and location and even location is filled in for them. The rest she reads from Q3 and shows back to confirm.
